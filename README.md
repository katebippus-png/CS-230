Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design? The Gaming Room was the client, and they wanted a web-based version of their game Draw It or Lose It. The goal was to make it scalable, support multiple teams/players, and run across different platforms instead of being limited to one system.

What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation? I think I did well organizing the document and clearly explaining the system design choices.

What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code? Working through the design document helped me plan everything before coding. It made the overall approach much clearer, which saved time later.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it? I would probably improve the detail in my system architecture section. I’d add more depth or visuals to better explain how everything connects.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing? I made sure to focus on what the client needed, like making the game easy to access, scalable, and able to support multiple players. I used those needs to guide my design decisions.

How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application? I approached the design by first understanding the requirements, then breaking everything into smaller, manageable pieces before planning the structure. In the future, I’d continue using this step-by-step approach along with Agile practices yo make the design more clear.
